# Design — Epic 01: Stabilize Θ

## Context

The `cognition` package's `HypergraphReasoner.reason()` currently uses Dempster's rule via `combine_multiple` to fuse the top-k decisive per-infon mass functions for a query (`reference_v2/src/cognition/logic.py:967–973`). On the released training configuration, the per-infon masses on the EV scenario become near-certain SUPPORTS during training, and Dempster-fusing several near-certain agreeing focal masses drives `m(Θ)` to ≈ 0 by construction. The audit's three pinned-seed reproduction (`paper_scenario_report.json`) shows this collapse is deterministic, not stochastic.

The paper's evidential-uncertainty story (H2) requires `m(Θ)` to remain non-trivial on thin-evidence claims. Either training has to leave per-infon masses with substantial Θ, or fusion has to preserve Θ from the masses it consumes — currently neither is happening at the default configuration.

## Goals / Non-Goals

- **Goals**: locate the collapse mechanically (training vs fusion); add three alternative fusion rules; add a coherence-weight sweep; add a top-k cap on fusion; pin seeds; produce a single named configuration that satisfies the acceptance criterion in `proposal.md`; write a short technical memo.
- **Non-Goals**: re-architecting the readout (4-mass DS stays); replacing the DS teacher; introducing new datasets (Epic 2); implementing baselines (Epic 3); paper drafting (Epic 4).

## Decisions

### Decision: Diagnose before patching

Before changing any code path, instrument the existing one and log per-infon masses pre-fusion for the Toyota and Honda queries (audit §Path 2.1). The fix space is different depending on whether per-infon masses already have `m(Θ) ≈ 0.002` (the collapse is in training and the fix lives in the coherence regularizer) or whether per-infon masses have `m(Θ) ≈ 0.20-0.30` and the fused result is degenerate (the fix lives in fusion).

**Why:** Patching speculatively across training, fusion, and top-k simultaneously will produce a combination that "works" without any single contributor being known. We need an audit trail in the technical memo that matches each intervention to the variance it explains.

---

### Decision: Add three fusion rules in addition to Dempster

Implement Yager's rule, Murphy averaging, and a `top1` cautious fusion in `dempster_shafer.py`, selectable via the existing `combine_multiple` interface or a sibling. Yager assigns Dempster-conflict mass to Θ (which is exactly the H2 semantics — when sources disagree we are *more* ignorant); Murphy averaging tempers the multiplicative combination; `top1` fuses nothing and returns the single most-decisive focal mass.

**Why:** Yager is the principled DS-theoretic answer to "we want conflict to land on Θ"; Murphy is the empirical workhorse known to avoid Zadeh's counter-example; `top1` is the trivial floor that tells us how much of the collapse is fusion's fault at all. All three are textbook (Yager 1987, Murphy 2000) so reviewers will recognize them.

**Alternatives considered:**
- *Replace Dempster everywhere*: too aggressive; breaks comparability with prior runs and removes Dempster as a sensible default.
- *Implement only Murphy*: insufficient evidence to choose without comparing.

---

### Decision: Sweep coherence weight, not modify the regularizer form

Sweep `coherence_weight ∈ {0, 0.2, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, 5.0}` (audit §Path 2.2) but keep the regularizer form `(1 − m(Θ)) · (1 − coherence)` unchanged.

**Why:** Coherence weight is a single scalar with an obvious dose–response interpretation; if the curve is monotone in `m(Θ)` then we can pick a defensible point on it. Changing the regularizer form simultaneously confounds the dose with the response.

---

### Decision: Cap top-k for fusion at 3, not 5

Add a configurable `decisive_top_k` with default 3 (current code: 5). Fusing 5 near-certain agreeing masses is the single biggest source of Θ → 0 by Dempster-rule arithmetic; capping at 3 lets us measure how much of the collapse is just over-eager fusion.

**Why:** Cheap to implement and physically interpretable. If `top1` fixes Θ, the right cap is small; if `top5` is needed for accuracy, we have a knob to tune the trade-off.

---

### Decision: Single canonical configuration committed

After the sweep, commit one named YAML config (e.g. `experiments/configs/canonical_v0_2.yaml`) and require all downstream papers/figures (Epics 2–4) to reference it by name. Per audit §Pre-submission, "regenerated from a single, named, seeded config" is the bar.

**Why:** Without a named config, "the paper's number" becomes ambiguous within months. With one, the entire paper can be regenerated by `python -m experiments.run --config canonical_v0_2.yaml`.

---

### Decision: Five seeds, not three

Run `{42, 0, 1, 7, 13}`. The audit used three seeds; for the canonical config we want five so the across-seed standard deviation is meaningful (n=3 std is noisy).

**Why:** Cost is small (each fit() is ~30 s on CPU); statistical power is meaningfully better at n=5. Also matches what reviewers expect: n ≥ 5 for a reported mean-and-std.

---

### Decision: Phase-1 memo, not paper section

The memo `docs/publication/phase1_collapse_fix.md` is for internal navigation and as raw material for Epic 4's paper. It is *not* the paper's Methods section — that gets written in Epic 4 after Epics 2 and 3 produce the supporting evidence.

**Why:** Premature drafting against an unreviewed code change locks the paper to whatever the first run looked like. The memo is the audit trail; the paper is the synthesis.

## Risks / Trade-offs

- **No configuration recovers Θ.** The contingency is documented in `proposal.md` Acceptance Criterion: H2 is retracted, Epics 2–4 are rescoped to an H1-only story. We pre-commit to that contingency rather than overfitting a config.
- **Yager + low coherence regularizer accidentally puts mass on Θ that is not "ignorance" but "conflict".** The two are formally the same in DS but reviewers will call out the conflation. Mitigation: report `m(Θ)` decomposed into "Θ from no-evidence" vs "Θ from conflict" using the coherence score as a proxy in the phase-1 memo.
- **Capping fusion at top-k=3 hurts polarity accuracy.** Mitigation: report polarity accuracy alongside `m(Θ)` for every config; only configs that preserve polarity advance.
- **Coherence weight ≥ 2.0 destabilizes training.** Mitigation: include a runtime sanity check (loss must decrease over first 5 epochs) and log warnings; record the failed configs in the memo.

## Open Questions

- Should the per-infon mass logger be a permanent feature or a debug-only path? **Recommendation:** permanent, gated by `Config.log_per_infon_masses`. It will be needed again in Epics 2 and 3.
- Is the activation-threshold drift (0.2 currently; audit §Path 3.1 hints the paper used something different) something we adjust now or in Epic 2? **Recommendation:** sweep at coarse resolution in this epic ({0.1, 0.2, 0.3}) only as a confound; full activation-threshold ablation happens in Epic 2 against the synthetic data.
