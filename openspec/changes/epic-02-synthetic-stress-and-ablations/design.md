# Design — Epic 02: Synthetic stress dataset + full ablation matrix

## Context

The 49-infon EV scenario is a useful illustrative example but a poor instrument: it has no orthogonal axes of variation, so no single number it produces can be interpreted as evidence about any particular hypothesis. Public benchmarks (Epic 03) introduce variation but also confounds — retrieval quality, annotator noise, evidence completeness. A synthetic corpus with planted oracle labels eliminates those confounds and lets us draw clean curves of `metric` vs `axis-of-variation`.

The two axes that matter for the paper:

- **H1 axis — compositional depth**: number of hops required to verify a query. Generated by chaining premises through known IKL operators; the oracle knows whether a query is 1-, 2-, 3-, or 4-hop because the generator built it that way.
- **H2 axis — evidence thinness**: number of supporting sentences in the corpus for a given claim. Generated by deciding, per query, how many redundant sentences witness the same triple; the oracle knows the exact count.

A third axis, **contradiction density**, is a confound for H2: it pushes Θ up via fusion conflict rather than via genuine ignorance. We need this axis specifically so we can show H2's effect is *not* just "Θ tracks conflict" — Spearman ρ(`m(Θ)`, thinness) at `contradiction_density=0` is the load-bearing measurement.

## Goals / Non-Goals

- **Goals**: deterministic synthetic generator with three orthogonal axes; full Cartesian ablation over the seven components in `spec.md`; publishable H1 effect-size table; publishable H2 correlation and AURC tables; reliability diagrams.
- **Non-Goals**: public benchmarks (Epic 03); LLM baselines (Epic 03); paper drafting (Epic 04); inventing new model components (Epic 01 already locked the canonical config).

## Decisions

### Decision: Generate from templates + IKL operators, not from natural language

The synthetic corpus is built by sampling templates parameterized over (anchor, anchor, anchor, polarity, tense, time) and emitting natural-looking sentences via a fixed templating function (e.g. "{actor} {relation} {feature} {tense_clause}"). It is *not* generated by an LLM.

**Why:** an LLM-generated synthetic corpus introduces an uncontrolled confound (LLM phrasing distribution) and makes the oracle labels unreliable — we cannot guarantee thinness or hop count if the LLM rewrites the sentence. Templates are crude but exact; for the purpose of testing the *graph* layer (which is what the hypotheses are about), templates are sufficient. We are not testing the SPLADE encoder's robustness here; that is a separate, well-studied question.

**Alternatives considered:**
- *LLM-augmented templates*: faster to write, but the oracle becomes fragile.
- *Real corpus (e.g. Wikipedia subset) with planted thinness*: real text but planted labels are unreliable on real text.

---

### Decision: Compositional depth via deterministic chain construction

A `compositional_depth=k` query is constructed by chaining `k` IKL premises (e.g. `(IF p1 THEN p2) AND (IF p2 THEN p3) AND p1 → p3`). The corpus contains exactly the sentences witnessing each premise, plus optional distractors. The oracle answer is determined by the chain's truth value at construction time.

**Why:** if we want to claim "typed-IKL aggregators outperform uniform-mean at higher hop counts" (H1), the only credible test is to give them queries the generator *knows* are k-hop and measure accuracy stratified by k. HoVer offers this in the wild but with noise; here we have it in the clean.

**Alternatives considered:** randomly sampling queries and post-hoc estimating their hop count — unreliable.

---

### Decision: Three seeds for the full ablation, five for the canonical cells

Run the full Cartesian ablation across 3 seeds (cost-bounded). For the eight named-canonical cells (the rows that go in the paper), re-run with 5 seeds.

**Why:** the matrix is large (~7 components × ~3 settings each = many cells); 3 seeds is sufficient for the matrix's role as a *map* of the design space. The paper's canonical numbers need 5 seeds for reportable mean ± std (matches Epic 01's seed policy).

---

### Decision: Paired bootstrap, not unpaired t-test

For pairwise comparisons (e.g. typed-IKL vs uniform-mean at `hop=4`), use paired bootstrap CI on per-scenario differences across the synthetic test set, not Welch's t-test on aggregate accuracies.

**Why:** the same scenarios are evaluated by both ablation arms; the paired structure is real and ignoring it loses statistical power. Welch's t-test on aggregates would also be parametric over a non-normal distribution.

---

### Decision: Held-out synthetic-test-thinness split

Generate `synthetic-train` (8 000 scenarios, used for any per-config training where transduction touches the corpus), `synthetic-dev` (1 000, used for ablation selection and hyperparameter peeking), and `synthetic-test-thinness` (1 000, with a *thinness curriculum*: stratified across `evidence_redundancy ∈ {1, 2, 5, 10}` so we can measure ρ across the entire range).

**Why:** the H2 correlation is the headline number; if we measure ρ on training-distribution-thinness, we are over-stating the signal. Holding out a thinness-curriculum test set ensures the reported ρ generalizes.

---

### Decision: Eight named cells from the matrix make the paper

Of the full Cartesian, only eight specific cells are pre-registered as the "rows" of the paper's ablation table:

1. **Canonical** — Epic 01's `canonical_v0_2.yaml` (4-mass DS readout, typed-IKL aggregator, fused with chosen rule, default coherence weight)
2. **Uniform aggregator** — replace typed-IKL with uniform-mean (R-GCN-style)
3. **Softmax readout** — replace 4-mass DS with 3-way softmax + temperature scaling, drop coherence regularizer
4. **Dirichlet (EDL) readout** — replace 4-mass DS with evidential Dirichlet (Sensoy 2018)
5. **Coherence-off** — set `coherence_weight=0`, all else canonical
6. **Teacher-only** — use teacher mass directly (no training); read out without the network
7. **Top-1 fusion** — `decisive_top_k=1`
8. **Single-layer** — one message-passing block instead of two

**Why eight specifically:** these cells are the minimum that adjudicate H1 (cells 2, 8 isolate the structure), H2 (cells 3, 4, 5 isolate the readout/regularizer), and the audit's open questions about training vs fusion (cells 6, 7).

---

### Decision: Ship a `[study]` extras group

Optional `pip install reference_v2[study]` adds `statsmodels` and `matplotlib` for the bootstrap and figure scripts. The base package keeps a small dep footprint.

**Why:** users running the production package don't need study-time deps; researchers reproducing the paper do.

## Risks / Trade-offs

- **Synthetic ≠ real**: a result on synthetic data may not transfer to AVeriTeC. Mitigation: Epic 03 verifies the headline H2/H1 effects on real data before the paper claims them.
- **Generator artifacts confounding ablations**: e.g. uniform aggregator might "win" at low hop counts because templates are too easy. Mitigation: stratify all reported numbers by hop count and thinness; never report a single aggregate.
- **10 000 scenarios may not be enough at 4-hop strata**: scenarios are stratified across many axes; 4-hop with `evidence_redundancy=1` may be a thin cell. Mitigation: monitor per-stratum count during pilot; oversample thin cells if needed.
- **Bootstrap CIs across non-IID synthetic scenarios may be optimistic**: scenarios share template machinery so dependence exists. Mitigation: also report seed-level standard deviation of the metrics, which captures generator-side dependence too.

## Open Questions

- Should the generator simulate retrieval failure (drop a supporting sentence with probability p)? **Tentative no** — that is closer to Epic 03's territory.
- Should `nei_fraction` be controlled at the corpus level or query level? **At the query level** — corpus-level NEI is a different construct (irrelevant docs). Query-level NEI (the corpus does not contain enough evidence for *this* query) is what the paper's H2 is about.
- Do we need to ablate the encoder (SPLADE → frozen-BERT)? **Not in this epic** — the encoder is a confound shared across all cells.
