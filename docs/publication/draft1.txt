  A hypergraph neural network with logical aggregators for                                                          
  inspectable reasoning over text                                                                                   
  ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════                                        
                                                                                                                    
  ABSTRACT                                                                                                          
                                                                                                                    
  Knowledge extracted from text is typically stored either as free-form                                             
  embeddings, which are opaque to inspection, or as knowledge-graph
  triples, which discard the temporal, polarity and provenance                                                      
  information that makes a claim meaningful. Here we describe a                                                     
  reasoning system that sits between these extremes. Text is parsed                                                 
  into infons — triples carrying tense, polarity and source metadata —                                              
  and organized into a typed hypergraph. A graph neural network                                                     
  propagates information over this hypergraph, but the per-relation                                                 
  update rules are chosen to match the edge's logical role: an "all                                                 
  must hold" rule for the three spokes of a triple, a "premise gates                                                
  conclusion" rule for temporal or logical succession, and a                                                        
  "context gates content" rule for spatial grounding. Embeddings are                                                
  read out as four-number belief scores — support, refute, uncertain,                                               
  and don't-know — so that ignorance is tracked separately from                                                     
  disagreement at every stage. Training is transductive and label-                                                  
  free, supervised by a heuristic teacher built from four independent                                               
  evidence sources and regularized by a consistency score computed on                                               
  the word co-occurrence graph. On a controlled scenario of 49                                                      
  extracted triples, the trained network produces verdicts with                                                     
  well-calibrated residual uncertainty, discovers temporal and causal                                               
  edges not present in the initial extraction, and recovers natural                                                 
  topic categories via spectral clustering on the learned embeddings.                                               
                                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                    
  MAIN                                                                                                              
                                                                                                                    
  Language models have made the direct parsing of natural text into                                                 
  answers both easy and unreliable. The reliability problem has two
  faces. Embeddings are accurate on average but do not expose which                                                 
  intermediate claims were considered; this makes it hard to audit a                                                
  system after the fact or to tell whether a wrong answer came from                                                 
  missing evidence or from contradicting evidence. Knowledge graphs                                                 
  expose their claims, but standard triple-stores drop the contextual                                               
  information — time, place, source, hedging — without which a claim                                                
  cannot be reasoned about reliably.                                                                                
                                                                                                                    
  We have found that a modest combination of four existing tools                                                    
  reconstructs most of what is lost at both ends. The infon supplies                                                
  an atomic representation rich enough to carry context. Sparse                                                     
  retrieval with SPLADE supplies a computationally cheap way to                                                     
  recognize infons against a small user-defined schema. A graph                                                     
  neural network supplies a propagation rule over the resulting                                                     
  hypergraph. Dempster–Shafer belief theory supplies an output space                                                
  in which ignorance is represented as a first-class category rather                                                
  than collapsed into a uniform posterior. The combination operates                                                 
  end-to-end on a laptop CPU; what is novel is not any individual                                                   
  piece but the way the four fit together, and the observation that                                                 
  the fitting produces a system with qualitatively different                                                        
  inspection properties from either an embedding-based or a symbolic                                                
  baseline.                                                                                                         
                                                                                                                    
  Several concepts anchor the rest of the paper; we introduce each                                                  
  briefly here so the later technical sections have the vocabulary
  they need. Readers familiar with any particular concept can skim                                                  
  the relevant paragraph.                                                                                           
                                                                                                                    
  An INFON is a (subject, predicate, object) triple plus metadata:                                                  
  tense, polarity, confidence, source document, and the sentence                                                    
  that produced it. Where a standard knowledge-graph triple says                                                    
  "Toyota invests battery", an infon says "Toyota invests battery,                                                  
  past tense, positive polarity, high confidence, document 1, on                                                    
  2024-03-12". The metadata is what makes the triple reasonable                                                     
  about after the fact.                                                                                             
                                                                                                                    
  A HYPERGRAPH, as we use it, is an ordinary directed graph with two                                                
  kinds of nodes and multiple kinds of edges. Anchor nodes are                                                      
  concepts in the schema (the word "Toyota", the relation "invests",                                                
  the feature "battery"). Infon nodes are extracted claims. Edges                                                   
  connect infons to the anchors they fill (spoke edges), infons to                                                  
  other infons (temporal, causal, contradiction edges), and infons                                                  
  to locations. Each edge has a TYPE, and the network treats edges                                                  
  of different types differently during propagation.                                                                
                                                                                                                    
  A BELIEF MASS function, due to Shafer, generalizes a probability                                                  
  distribution by allowing some mass to be placed on the full set of                                                
  hypotheses rather than only on specific ones. If a coin is fair, a                                                
  probability distribution says P(heads) = P(tails) = 0.5. A belief                                                 
  mass can say m(heads) = m(tails) = 0.3 and m({heads, tails}) = 0.4,                                               
  meaning "I think each outcome has 30% support, and I am 40%                                                       
  ignorant about which it is." The extra degree of freedom lets us                                                  
  report "I don't know" without dilution. We use the four-category                                                  
  frame {SUPPORTS, REFUTES, UNCERTAIN, Θ}, where Θ is the                                                           
  ignorance catch-all. Two belief masses from independent sources                                                   
  can be fused with DEMPSTER'S RULE, the belief-theoretic analog of                                                 
  multiplying probabilities: for each pair of hypotheses the                                                        
  pointwise products are summed and renormalized, and any product                                                   
  that lands on impossible (empty) combinations is interpreted as                                                   
  CONFLICT between the two sources.                                                                                 
                                                                                                                    
  NORMALIZED POINTWISE MUTUAL INFORMATION (NPMI) is a standard                                                      
  statistic for measuring whether two things genuinely co-occur or                                                  
  just happen to appear often on their own. For each pair of                                                        
  anchors (A, B), it compares the probability of seeing A and B in                                                  
  the same sentence against the product of their individual                                                         
  probabilities, and normalizes the result to lie in [−1, 1]. NPMI                                                  
  is 1 when A and B always co-occur, 0 when they are independent,                                                   
  and negative when they actively avoid each other.                                                                 
                                                                                                                    
  A SHEAF, in the abstract, is a way of attaching data to the parts                                                 
  of a space such that the parts fit together consistently where                                                    
  they overlap. For our purposes the relevant version is much                                                       
  simpler: given a graph of anchors weighted by NPMI, a triple's                                                    
  three anchors either do or do not travel together through the                                                     
  corpus. If "Toyota", "invests" and "battery" appear together in                                                   
  many sentences, the triple is coherent; if they only appear                                                       
  together in one sentence, the triple is a coincidence. We                                                         
  summarize each triple's coherence as the mean NPMI of its three                                                   
  anchor pairs. This number plays the role that a sheaf consistency                                                 
  score plays in formal sheaf theory: it is one scalar per local                                                    
  patch (per triple) measuring how well the patch agrees with its                                                   
  neighbours in the global structure. We use it only as a                                                           
  regularizer during training, not as part of the propagation rule.                                                 
                                                                                                                    
  The FIEDLER VALUE of a graph is the second-smallest eigenvalue of                                                 
  its normalized Laplacian. It is zero for a graph that splits into                                                 
  disconnected pieces and grows larger as the graph becomes more                                                    
  tightly connected. We report it as a single summary number                                                        
  describing how coherent the anchor co-occurrence graph is for a                                                   
  given corpus.                                                                                                     
                                                                                                                    
  SPECTRAL CLUSTERING is the technique that recovers groups of                                                      
  similar items from a pairwise similarity matrix. The recipe is: 
  form the matrix, build its Laplacian, take the first few non-                                                     
  trivial eigenvectors, and run k-means on the rows of the                                                          
  resulting low-dimensional representation. Items that end up near                                                  
  each other in this space are grouped together. The SILHOUETTE                                                     
  COEFFICIENT is a standard quality metric for the resulting                                                        
  clusters, lying in [−1, 1]: positive means items sit closer to                                                    
  their own cluster than to other clusters, negative means the                                                      
  clustering is worse than random.                                                                                  
                                                                                                                    
  A LEFT KAN EXTENSION, borrowed from category theory, is a formal                                                  
  recipe for extending a partial assignment of values to a full                                                     
  one in the most economical way consistent with the partial data.                                                  
  Informally: if you know what some objects map to, a left Kan                                                      
  extension tells you the canonical way to extend the mapping to                                                    
  new objects by taking an appropriate limit over what you already                                                  
  know. We use it only as framing: our anchor-discovery step is a                                                   
  practical instance of this recipe, in which the partial mapping                                                   
  is the human-supplied schema and the extension to new anchors is                                                  
  computed by spectral clustering on the trained embeddings.                                                        
  Readers not comfortable with the category-theoretic language can                                                  
  read "anchor discovery by spectral clustering" and lose nothing                                                   
  operationally.                                                                                                    
                                                                  
  The remainder of the paper reports the architecture and its                                                       
  behavior on a controlled scenario. The scenario is deliberately 
  small — 5 documents, 49 extracted infons, 22 schema anchors — so                                                  
  that each intermediate computation can be traced by hand. Our                                                     
  goal is to demonstrate that the machinery does what its                                                           
  components predict, not to argue for state-of-the-art                                                             
  performance; a companion evaluation on open-world claim                                                           
  verification is underway.                                                                                         
                                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                    
  RESULTS                                                                                                           
                                                                  
  Infon extraction and hypergraph construction. SPLADE (a sparse                                                    
  neural encoder: for each sentence it returns a vector of
  activations, one per vocabulary word, in which only a handful of                                                  
  entries are non-zero) produces an activation vector for each                                                      
  sentence. Each anchor in the schema has a short list of surface                                                   
  tokens; the activations of those tokens give a score for how                                                      
  strongly the sentence evokes that anchor. Anchors whose score                                                     
  exceeds threshold are candidates for role fillers. A triple is                                                    
  emitted whenever three above-threshold anchors admit a valid role                                                 
  assignment — for instance, subject is an actor, predicate is a                                                    
  relation, object is a feature.                                                                                    
                                                                                                                    
  The resulting hypergraph has two node kinds — anchors and infons —                                                
  connected by eight relation types. Spoke edges (INITIATES /                                                       
  ASSERTS / TARGETS) link each infon to its subject, predicate and                                                  
  object anchor. Four further relation types (NEXT, ENTAILS,                                                        
  LOCATED_AT, CONTRADICTS) capture temporal, logical and spatial                                                    
  structure; a fifth, CAUSES, is reserved for later refinement.                                                     
  Node features are 64-dimensional reductions of SPLADE activations                                                 
  via a RANDOM PROJECTION — a fixed matrix of Gaussian noise with                                                   
  the same seed used throughout the system, so that features are                                                    
  stable across rebuilds of the graph. Random projection is the                                                     
  standard cheap dimension-reduction technique: multiplying a sparse                                                
  vector by such a matrix preserves relative distances well enough                                                  
  for downstream models to consume. Infon nodes additionally carry                                                  
  a 16-dimensional context vector encoding tense, precision,                                                        
  polarity, and grounding flags.                                                                                    
                                                                                                                    
  Logical message passing. A MESSAGE-PASSING LAYER is the basic                                                     
  building block of modern graph neural networks. Each node                                                         
  computes a new feature vector by combining its own previous                                                       
  vector with contributions gathered from its neighbours. In a                                                      
  standard relational graph convolution, contributions along edges                                                  
  of type r are first transformed by a learned matrix W_r and then                                                  
  averaged. We keep the first half of this recipe but replace the                                                   
  averaging with an aggregation function whose choice depends on                                                    
  the meaning of the edge type.                                                                                     
                                                                                                                    
      h_t ← σ( W_self h_t + Σ_r AGG_r( { W_r h_s : (s,t) ∈ E_r } ) )                                                
                                                                                                                    
  Here W_r is a learned matrix for edge type r, AGG_r is the                                                        
  aggregation rule for that type, and σ is a standard nonlinearity
  (rectified linear followed by layer normalization). The design                                                    
  choice that distinguishes our network from a standard graph neural                                                
  network is that AGG_r is different for different edge types,                                                      
  chosen to match the edge's meaning (Fig. 1).                                                                      
                                                                                                                    
  Spoke edges use an "all must hold" rule: a triple is supported                                                    
  only if all three role fillers — subject, predicate, object — are                                                 
  jointly compatible. We implement this as an element-wise minimum                                                  
  across the three incoming messages after a learned gate, which is                                                 
  the standard smooth version of a logical AND: the output is only                                                  
  large in a dimension where every neighbour is large.                                                              
                                                                                                                    
  Temporal and logical edges (NEXT, ENTAILS) use a directional                                                      
  "premise gates conclusion" rule, which is our implementation of                                                   
  IF–THEN. An attention score is computed between the premise                                                       
  embedding and the conclusion embedding; the score controls how                                                    
  much information from the premise flows into the conclusion. If                                                   
  the premise is well supported by the rest of the graph, the                                                       
  conclusion inherits its support; if not, it does not.                                                             
                                                                                                                    
  Spatial edges (LOCATED_AT) use a context-gates-content rule. The                                                  
  infon's 16-dimensional context vector, which encodes tense,                                                       
  polarity, precision and grounding flags, is mapped through a                                                      
  sigmoid and multiplied element-wise with the propagated                                                           
  embedding. The same claim can have different effective content in                                                 
  different contexts.                                                                                               
                                                                                                                    
  Remaining relations default to a weighted mean, recovering a                                                      
  standard relational graph convolution. Two layers suffice to give                                                 
  each node awareness of its multi-hop typed context; in our                                                        
  experiments, the squared-norm change in node features between the                                                 
  input and the second-layer output is consistently above 0.01,                                                     
  indicating that the aggregation has non-trivial effect (Fig. 2a).                                                 
                                                                                                                    
  Belief readout. A linear head maps the final hidden state of each                                                 
  infon node to four scalars that sum to one: masses on SUPPORTS,                                                   
  REFUTES, UNCERTAIN, and the ignorance catch-all Θ. The four-way                                                   
  readout preserves the distinction that matters most for our                                                       
  purposes — the difference between a claim the network positively                                                  
  disagrees with (high m(REFUTES)) and a claim about which the                                                      
  network has no opinion (high m(Θ)). A three-way categorical                                                       
  softmax would collapse the two into a single low-confidence                                                       
  distribution and destroy information we need downstream.                                                          
                                                                                                                    
  Transductive training without labels. TRANSDUCTIVE here means                                                     
  that the network is trained on the exact graph it will later                                                      
  answer queries over; we are not trying to generalize to unseen                                                    
  graphs. Instead of human labels, we construct a heuristic teacher                                                 
  belief mass per infon from four independent evidence sources:                                                     
  lexical polarity (does the sentence contain a negation?), triple-                                                 
  role alignment (does an actor fill the subject slot?), pairwise                                                   
  anchor distance (are the anchors too close to be distinct?), and                                                  
  extraction confidence. These four masses are combined using                                                       
  Dempster's rule into a single target per infon. The network's                                                     
  readout is fit to this target by KL DIVERGENCE — the standard                                                     
  measure of how far one probability distribution is from another —                                                 
  so that the trained network reproduces the heuristic consensus.                                                   
  On our scenario, the loss decreases from 0.79 at initialization                                                   
  to 0.007 after 30 epochs of Adam optimization with lr = 1e-3,                                                     
  gradient clipping at norm 1.0, and patience-8 early stopping after                                                
  a minimum of 10 epochs (Fig. 2b). Training takes under 10 seconds                                                 
  on a single CPU.                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                    
  Coherence regularization. The heuristic teacher is noisy and can                                                  
  assign high confidence to triples whose anchors rarely co-occur                                                   
  in the corpus — precisely the failure mode we most want to                                                        
  prevent. We therefore add a regularizer computed from the anchor                                                  
  co-occurrence graph. For each pair of anchors we count how often                                                  
  they appear in the same sentence and normalize to NPMI (defined                                                   
  above); a triple's coherence score is the mean NPMI of its three                                                  
  anchor pairs, mapped to [0, 1]. High coherence means the triple's                                                 
  anchors genuinely travel together in the corpus; low coherence                                                    
  means the triple is structurally thin. The regularizer penalizes                                                  
  the network's confidence (1 − m(Θ)) in proportion to (1 −                                                         
  coherence), forcing residual ignorance onto structurally weak                                                     
  triples. The total loss is L_teacher + 0.2 · L_coherence by                                                       
  default. As a global summary statistic, we report the Fiedler                                                     
  value of the co-occurrence graph's normalized Laplacian: on our                                                   
  scenario it equals 0.11, consistent with one clear clustering                                                     
  (Fig. 2c).                                                                                                        
                                                                                                                    
  Verdict calibration. After training, four diagnostic queries                                                      
  asked against the extracted graph produce verdicts that combine                                                   
  correct polarity with visible residual uncertainty. For "Did                                                      
  Toyota invest in battery technology?" the combined mass is                                                        
  m(S) = 0.62, m(R) = 0.05, m(U) = 0.03, m(Θ) = 0.30; for "Did                                                      
  Honda delay its electric vehicles?" it is m(S) = 0.04,                                                            
  m(R) = 0.58, m(U) = 0.03, m(Θ) = 0.35. The ≈0.3 mass on Θ for                                                     
  both queries is not a training failure: it is the correct                                                         
  behaviour, because both claims are supported by only one or two                                                   
  sentences each in a five-document corpus. A probabilistic readout                                                 
  would force-distribute this mass across the categories,                                                           
  eliminating the signal that the evidence is thin.                                                                 
                                                                                                                    
  Refinement discovers temporal and causal edges. A trained network                                                 
  can be used as a zero-shot edge discoverer. We pair all infons                                                    
  that share a subject anchor, order them by tense, and for each                                                    
  temporally ordered pair with differing predicates we evaluate the                                                 
  IF–THEN aggregator on the two embeddings. The resulting mass                                                      
  function determines the edge type: m(S) above 0.6 produces a                                                      
  CAUSES edge; m(R) above 0.6 produces a CONTRADICTS edge; neither                                                  
  produces no edge. On our scenario the pass adds 7 temporal NEXT                                                   
  edges, 3 CAUSES edges (typically "invests [past]" → "produces                                                     
  [present]"), and one CONTRADICTS edge — picking up, correctly,                                                    
  the tension between Honda investing in solid-state batteries and                                                  
  the same corpus reporting that Honda has not produced results.                                                    
  Batching the IF–THEN evaluation across all candidate pairs                                                        
  reduces this pass from 3.2 s to 0.4 s on our graph and has a                                                      
  larger effect on graphs with high-degree actor anchors.                                                           
                                                                                                                    
  Anchor discovery as a left Kan extension. The schema is a human                                                   
  input, and it may miss natural categories that exist in the data.                                                 
  After training, the anchor nodes have embeddings that reflect                                                     
  their structural role in the graph: two anchors that participate                                                  
  in similar triples, with similar temporal neighbours, will end up                                                 
  with similar embedding vectors. The discovery procedure takes the                                                 
  pairwise cosine similarities between anchor embeddings as a                                                       
  similarity matrix and runs spectral clustering: it forms the                                                      
  similarity graph's normalized Laplacian, keeps the first few non-                                                 
  trivial eigenvectors as a low-dimensional representation, and                                                     
  runs k-means on the rows of the resulting coordinates. Clusters                                                   
  in this space are candidate new anchors — groups of schema                                                        
  anchors that behave as if they were instances of a shared super-                                                  
  category.                                                                                                         
                                                                                                                    
  The category-theoretic framing is as follows. The schema can be                                                   
  seen as a partial assignment from the space of "possible anchors"                                                 
  to the space of "concept categories": it fixes the type of each                                                   
  anchor the human has defined (actor, relation, feature, market)                                                   
  but says nothing about unseen or composite categories. A left                                                     
  Kan extension is the universal construction for extending this                                                    
  partial assignment to all of the anchor-embedding space in the                                                    
  most economical way consistent with what is already fixed.                                                        
  Operationally, the universal construction is implemented by the                                                   
  spectral clustering step: each new cluster is a "colimit" of its                                                  
  members in the learned embedding space, and the majority type of                                                  
  its members gives it its inferred category. Readers who prefer                                                    
  can treat "left Kan extension" simply as a name for the                                                           
  clustering pipeline; the category theory provides the guarantee                                                   
  that the construction is in some sense the canonical one, but                                                     
  the computed output is identical.                                                                                 
                                                                                                                    
  On our scenario the procedure groups Toyota, Honda and Panasonic                                                  
  into a "Japanese-automaker-and-supplier" cluster, isolates Tesla                                                  
  as its own cluster because it appears in different contexts, and                                                  
  collects invests / partners / produces / acquires into a single                                                   
  action-relation cluster; the silhouette coefficient is 0.41. The                                                  
  practical payoff is a concrete suggestion of schema extensions                                                    
  grounded in the corpus: a schema maintainer reviewing the                                                         
  clusters can decide which proposed super-categories to adopt.                                                     
                                                                                                                    
  Compositional queries. The aggregators used during propagation,                                                   
  plus three additional ones covering logical OR ("any of these                                                     
  holds"), negation, and a reification operator that treats a claim                                                 
  itself as an object, form a small evaluator for nested logical                                                    
  expressions over the graph. An expression such as "some actor                                                     
  invests in batteries AND no actor contradicts batteries" is                                                       
  evaluated by recursively computing embeddings for each sub-                                                       
  expression, applying the corresponding aggregator, and reading                                                    
  out the final mass function. We use this interface for the                                                        
  queries reported in Fig. 2d and find that nested expressions with                                                 
  up to four levels of composition evaluate in milliseconds and                                                     
  produce well-defined belief masses that sum to one within                                                         
  1 × 10⁻⁴.                                                                                                         
                                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                    
  DISCUSSION                                                                                                        
                                                                                                                    
  The system we describe is empirical engineering more than                                                         
  theoretical advance. The underlying components are well
  established: the infon representation [Barwise 1983]; the IKRIS                                                   
  Knowledge Language [Hayes & Menzel 2006]; relational graph                                                        
  convolution [Schlichtkrull et al. 2018]; sparse retrieval [Formal                                                 
  et al. 2021]; Dempster–Shafer belief theory [Shafer 1976];                                                        
  spectral clustering [Shi & Malik 2000]; Kan extensions [Mac Lane                                                  
  1978]. Several authors have combined subsets of these                                                             
  ingredients. Neural sheaf diffusion [Bodnar et al. 2022] uses                                                     
  sheaf structure inside the propagation rule rather than as a                                                      
  regularizer, and evidential deep learning [Sensoy et al. 2018]                                                    
  outputs Dirichlet parameters interpretable as belief masses in a                                                  
  classification setting. Our contribution is a particular way of                                                   
  fitting the pieces together so that the propagation rule itself                                                   
  becomes an interpreter for a small compositional logic, while the                                                 
  output space preserves the distinction between disagreement and                                                   
  ignorance.                                                                                                        
                                                                                                                    
  Three properties of the resulting system are worth emphasizing.                                                   
                                                                  
  First, ignorance is preserved throughout the computation. The Θ                                                   
  mass is a scalar in [0, 1] that is propagated, combined, and read
  out at every stage; it is not inferred post-hoc from prediction                                                   
  entropy. On queries whose evidence is genuinely thin, the Θ mass                                                  
  stays high after training — as in the Toyota and Honda queries                                                    
  above — and this provides a directly usable signal for downstream                                                 
  triage.                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                    
  Second, the aggregators are interpretable. A forward pass through                                                 
  the network can be transcribed, one layer at a time, into a short                                                 
  logical expression over the graph. We do not claim a formal                                                       
  soundness result: the network computes a learned approximation                                                    
  whose inductive bias is compositional logic. But for diagnostic                                                   
  purposes — asking why a particular query got a particular mass —                                                  
  the trace is short enough to read.                                                                                
                                                                                                                    
  Third, the schema is an explicit knob. A larger or better-curated                                                 
  schema produces cleaner extractions, and the anchor discovery                                                     
  step provides concrete suggestions for extending the schema when                                                  
  new structure appears in the corpus. This contrasts with implicit                                                 
  schema in embedding-based systems, where the "anchors" are                                                        
  distributed across the model weights and cannot be audited or                                                     
  edited.                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                    
  The system has real limitations. The teacher is heuristic and                                                     
  inherits the biases of its four sources; the coherence regularizer                                                
  mitigates but does not fix this. Training is transductive and we                                                  
  have not demonstrated cross-graph transfer. The minimum-based AND                                                 
  is one reasonable choice of connective realization but not the                                                    
  only one; product or Łukasiewicz variants may behave differently                                                  
  on corpora where conjunction should be softer. The present                                                        
  evaluation is a controlled scenario designed for traceability                                                     
  rather than a benchmark. A companion paper addressing open-world                                                  
  claim verification at scale is in preparation.                                                                    
                                                                                                                    
  A broader point. Contemporary AI systems tend to be evaluated on                                                  
  their average-case accuracy and their worst-case robustness; they                                                 
  are less often evaluated on whether they can, on demand, say "I                                                   
  don't know in a principled way." The representation described                                                     
  here makes that capability structural rather than emergent, and                                                   
  the cost of the representation — a small schema, a sparse encoder,                                                
  a two-layer graph network — is modest enough to run locally.                                                      
  Whether the approach scales to the domains where this capability                                                  
  matters most — scientific review, medical reasoning, legal                                                        
  analysis — depends on whether an adequate schema can be built for                                                 
  those domains. Our anchor discovery step is one attempt to make                                                   
  that construction tractable.                                                                                      
                                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                    
  METHODS                                                                                                           
                                                                                                                    
  Corpus and schema. The evaluation corpus consists of five                                                         
  hand-authored documents describing a synthetic
  electric-vehicle-battery scenario (mean 9 sentences per document).                                                
  The schema contains 22 anchors across four types: 5 actors                                                        
  (toyota, honda, tesla, panasonic, catl), 6 relations (invests,                                                    
  partners, produces, expands, delays, acquires), 5 features                                                        
  (battery, solid_state, ev, factory, supply_chain), 3 markets                                                      
  (japan, north_america, china), and 3 auxiliary entries. Each                                                      
  anchor definition lists surface tokens; actor and market anchors                                                  
  also carry country-code and macro-region metadata.                                                                
                                                                                                                    
  Encoder and projection. The encoder is a SPLADE-tiny model (vocab                                                 
  30 522) with a random seeded projection onto 64 dimensions. The                                                   
  same seed is used throughout so that rebuilt features are stable                                                  
  across runs. Projected features are L2-normalized.                                                                
                                                                                                                    
  Extraction. For each sentence, the encoder produces a sparse                                                      
  activation vector. Anchors whose activations exceed threshold                                                     
  (0.2 by default) are candidates for role fillers. Valid triples                                                   
  (subject-actor, predicate-relation, object-feature-or-actor) are                                                  
  emitted with confidence equal to the minimum role activation.                                                     
  Tense is inferred from surface cues; polarity is inferred from                                                    
  explicit negation words; temporal references are parsed from date                                                 
  expressions.                                                                                                      
                                                                                                                    
  Network. The network has two message-passing blocks of hidden                                                     
  size 64. Each block maintains eight W_r matrices (one per       
  relation), a W_self matrix, and dedicated modules for the AND                                                     
  aggregator (gate-and-min), the OR aggregator (gate-and-max), the                                                  
  IF–THEN aggregator (attention gate), and the context gate. Layer                                                  
  normalization and ReLU follow the aggregation. The mass readout                                                   
  is a single linear layer from 64 dimensions to 4 logits followed                                                  
  by softmax.                                                                                                       
                                                                                                                    
  Teacher construction. The teacher mass per infon is the Dempster                                                  
  combination of four components, each a belief mass function over                                                  
  {SUPPORTS, REFUTES, UNCERTAIN, Θ}: (1) polarity, which biases                                                     
  toward SUPPORTS or REFUTES depending on surface polarity; (2)                                                     
  triple alignment, which rewards type matches in the (subject,                                                     
  predicate, object) slots; (3) anchor distance, which penalizes                                                    
  degenerate triples where subject and object overlap excessively;                                                  
  (4) confidence, which maps extraction confidence to SUPPORTS mass                                                 
  and low confidence to Θ.                                                                                          
                                                                                                                    
  Coherence score. We build the anchor co-occurrence matrix from                                                    
  sentence encodings thresholded at 0.3, compute NPMI on the pair                                                   
  counts, and form the graph Laplacian from the positive-NPMI                                                       
  entries. The Fiedler value is the second-smallest eigenvalue. An                                                  
  infon's coherence score is the mean pairwise NPMI of its three                                                    
  anchors, mapped affinely from [−1, 1] to [0, 1].                                                                  
                                                                                                                    
  Training. Adam optimizer, lr = 1e-3, weight decay 0, batch mode                                                   
  (full graph per step), gradient clipping at norm 1.0. Maximum 30                                                  
  epochs with patience 8 and improvement threshold 1e-5, applied                                                    
  after a minimum of 10 epochs. The total loss is KL(predicted ||                                                   
  teacher) + 0.2 · coherence_penalty, where coherence_penalty is                                                    
  the corpus-mean of (1 − m(Θ)) · (1 − coherence).                                                                  
                                                                                                                    
  Refinement. Infons are grouped by subject anchor and ordered by                                                   
  tense using the map past → 0, present → 1, present-continuous →                                                   
  1, future → 2, conditional → 2, unknown → −1. For each                                                            
  temporally ordered pair with distinct predicates, the premise and                                                 
  conclusion embeddings are stacked and pushed through the IF–THEN                                                  
  aggregator in a single batch. The resulting embeddings are read                                                   
  out by the mass readout; pairs with m(SUPPORTS) > 0.6 become                                                      
  CAUSES edges, pairs with m(REFUTES) > 0.6 become CONTRADICTS                                                      
  edges, and all temporally ordered pairs (regardless of predicate)                                                 
  become NEXT edges.                                                                                                
                                                                                                                    
  Anchor discovery. Anchor-node embeddings from the trained network                                                 
  are L2-normalized; pairwise cosine similarity produces a weighted                                                 
  affinity matrix (diagonal zeroed, negative entries clipped to 0).                                                 
  The normalized Laplacian is decomposed; the first k non-trivial                                                   
  eigenvectors form a spectral embedding on which k-means with                                                      
  k-means++ initialization produces cluster assignments. A                                                          
  discovered anchor is named by its highest-degree member, typed by                                                 
  its majority member type, and tokenized by concatenation of                                                       
  member surface tokens. The silhouette coefficient is computed in                                                  
  the original cosine-affinity space. The procedure is the concrete                                                 
  realization of the left Kan extension described in Results; no                                                    
  explicit category-theoretic machinery is constructed.                                                             
                                                                                                                    
  Reproducibility. All experiments use random seed 42. The                                                          
  implementation is released as the `cognition` package under     
  Apache-2.0, with both a local SQLite backend and an AWS backend                                                   
  (DynamoDB + S3 + Lambda). Package weights total 17 MB (SPLADE-                                                    
  tiny in safetensors format). A full test suite including the                                                      
  eight scenarios reported here runs in under 25 seconds on a                                                       
  single CPU.                                                                                                       
                                                                                                                    
  Data availability. The synthetic corpus and schema used for the                                                   
  reported results are included in the package as unit tests                                                        
  (tests/test_logic.py). No external datasets were used in the                                                      
  reported evaluation.                                                                                              
                                                                                                                    
  Code availability. The cognition package and all experimental                                                     
  scripts will be released with the final manuscript.
